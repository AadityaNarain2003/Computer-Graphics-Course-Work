Q1. Why canâ€™t we render point and directional lights with uniform hemisphere sampling or cosine
weighted sampling?
Ans: We can't render point and directional lights with any hemisphere sampling because the point is a very small specific point in the 3D space and same goes for the direction as it is very specific, therefore it becomes a 0 probablity event to hit that same position or direction. Therefore it doesnt work.

Q2.Why does the noise increase for the same number of samples in the case of uniform hemisphere
and cosine weighted sampling as the size of the area light decreases?
Ans: As during a hemisphere sampling the sampling is done for the hemisphere and does not consider light source. So as area decreases, so does the probablity of hitting the light, hence more noise.
